Knowledge Check

Pick the best answer for each. Explanations appear after submit. 80% required for BG maintenance service sign-off.

Question 1

What makes a BG recommendation feel like advice instead of an upsell?

Correct: Tie it to evidence. The trigger is what gives the recommendation credibility.
Question 2

Which is a strong trigger for a BG fuel service?

Correct: GDI, symptoms, or fuel-economy concern. BG identifies GDI carbon deposits, reduced power, reduced fuel economy, and yearly/15,000-mile maintenance as fuel-service triggers.
Question 3

What is the best customer explanation for a BG transmission service?

Correct: Explain heat, fluid, and protection. That is practical and avoids fear language.
Question 4

When is a BG brake fluid service especially easy to justify?

Correct: Condition and repair context matter. Brake fluid recommendations should be tied to test results, condition, time/mileage, pedal feel, or brake work.
Question 5

The customer asks, "Is this required?" Best response?

Correct: Be honest and specific. Customers trust the advisor who distinguishes required repairs from recommended maintenance.
Question 6

Which statement about BG protection coverage is safest?

Correct: Verify eligibility. Coverage can be valuable, but the advisor must confirm the local program requirements before promising anything.
Question 7

Where do most BG services belong in a Good / Better / Best menu?

Correct: Usually Better or Best. BG services are often preventive, so menu placement should match the vehicle finding and customer priority.
Question 8

What should be documented if a customer declines a BG service?

Correct: Document the reason. The next advisor should be able to restart the conversation without guessing.
Question 9

Which is a clean trigger for a BG cooling system service?

Correct: Coolant condition, time, testing, and repair context. Those are practical, defensible reasons to recommend it.
Question 10

What is the best overall BG advisor standard?

Correct: Keep it practical. The best BG recommendation is fact-based, clear, optional, and documented.
