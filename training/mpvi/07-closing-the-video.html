Closing the Video & Handoff

The last 10 seconds of your video matter almost as much as the first 10. A strong close hands the customer back to the advisor cleanly. A weak close kills the trust you just built.

Lesson Objective

Close every video with a consistent, confident handoff that sets the advisor up to win — and pass the right notes back so they can take it from there.

The Three-Part Close

  1. Recap. One sentence summary of what you showed.
  2. Handoff to the advisor. Name the advisor. Let the customer know who's calling next.
  3. Confidence statement. One line that closes calmly. Not a pitch — just a friendly close.

Word Tracks

Standard close (one finding):

"So that's the inside-edge wear on your front tires. Your advisor — Mike — is going to give you a call and walk you through the options. Appreciate you bringing it in. We'll take care of you."

Multi-finding close:

"To recap — the rear brakes are at about 3 millimeters, your battery is on the older side, and that cabin filter is past due. Sarah will give you a call with everything laid out and you can decide what works for you. Thanks for trusting us with your car."

All-green close:

"That's it — everything checked out solid on your Camry. No work needed today, just the maintenance you came in for. Mike will give you a quick call when we're wrapping up. Appreciate you, Mr. Patel."

Customer concern resolved:

"So that was the source of that noise you were hearing — the warped rotor we just looked at. Sarah will go over your options for getting that taken care of. Thanks for letting us know about it."

What NOT to Say at the End

"So... yeah... that's about it."

Trail-off ending. Customer doesn't know what's next. Sounds unsure.

"You're going to want to get this fixed ASAP or something bad will happen."

Pressure language at the close undoes all the trust you built. Stay calm.

"This is going to be expensive but it has to be done."

Price is the advisor's job. You don't quote, you don't pre-qualify, you don't soften the price for them. Hand it off clean.

"I don't know what they're going to charge but it'll be a lot."

Same issue. You just torpedoed the advisor's conversation before it started.

Pass the Right Notes Back

The video is half the handoff. The other half is the notes you send to the advisor. After every MPVI:

  • Confirm what's on the video. List the findings briefly so the advisor doesn't have to watch from start to finish to know what's in there.
  • Add what's not on the video. If you spotted something minor and didn't include it in the video, note it so the advisor can mention it or save for next visit.
  • Flag urgency level. Green / yellow / red on each item, same as on the video.
  • Note anything the advisor should know that the customer shouldn't hear. Prior decline patterns, mismatched tires, signs of an aftermarket fix.

Sample handoff note

VIDEO MPVI NOTES — [Tech Initials] [Date]

ON VIDEO:
  - Front tires inside edge wear (yellow) — 4/32 inside, 6/32 center
  - Rear brakes (red) — 2mm front, recommend immediate
  - Cabin filter (yellow) — overdue, dust heavy

NOT ON VIDEO (for advisor awareness):
  - Aftermarket exhaust installed, looks recent — not a concern but
    customer may ask about emissions

URGENCY: 1 red, 2 yellow
CUSTOMER NOTES: Mentioned planning a road trip in 2 weeks

Three minutes to type that note. Two hours of advisor time saved.

The Handoff Checklist

Manager Coaching Tip

Review the last 10 seconds of every video for a week. If the close trails off or sounds unsure, it's coachable in 30 seconds. The three-part close is the easiest part of the video to fix and one of the highest-leverage. Consistent closes across the team = customers feeling consistent quality across every visit.
