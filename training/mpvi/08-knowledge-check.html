Knowledge Check

Pick the best answer for each. You'll see your score and explanations once you submit. 80% is required to be signed off on the video MPVI standard at Dyer.

Question 1

What's the target length for a Dyer MPVI video?

Correct: 90 seconds or less. Long enough to show the findings clearly, short enough that customers actually finish watching.
Question 2

How many findings should be in a single video?

Correct: No more than three. A focused video on three findings sells. A long video on twelve overwhelms the customer and gets skipped.
Question 3

Which is the right way to open every Dyer MPVI video?

Correct: Four-part opening. Tech's name, the customer's vehicle, the finding, and the bridge into what you're showing. Same structure every time — customers learn to trust it.
Question 4

What's the single most common reason MPVI videos fail to convert?

Correct: The customer can't see it. Too far, too dark, no hand on the finding. If the customer can't see it, they don't believe it — and they don't approve the work.
Question 5

When showing a brake pad, what should fill most of the frame?

Correct: The pad and caliper with your finger pointing at the friction material. The finding fills the frame. Your hand makes it personal and clear.
Question 6

What does a "yellow" finding mean in the green/yellow/red system?

Correct: A watch item. Yellow = wearing but not failing yet. Most GP per RO opportunity lives in yellow items the customer understands and approves before they become red.
Question 7

You found 1 green item, 1 yellow, and 1 red on a car. What order should you show them?

Correct: Green, yellow, red. Starting with what's healthy builds trust before you bring up what needs attention. Customers stay engaged and the recommendation lands cleaner.
Question 8

When showing a dirty cabin filter on video, what's the most effective comparison?

Correct: Old next to new in the same frame. The visual contrast does the selling. The customer doesn't know what "clean" looks like unless you show them.
Question 9

Which closing line follows the Dyer standard?

Correct: Option A. Recap, hand off to the advisor by name, calm confidence close. No pressure language, no pricing, no trail-off.
Question 10

What is NOT the technician's job during the MPVI?

Correct: Quoting prices. That's the advisor's job. Stay in your lane — show the finding, name the urgency honestly, hand off clean. Pricing the work on camera undermines the advisor and the whole process.
